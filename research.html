This lesson is designed to help students conduct effective academic research. After completing this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Search for relevant academic papers and articles
  • Evaluate sources for credibility
  • Organize your research and cite sources correctly

Search for Relevant Research

Start by using academic databases such as Google Scholar, JSTOR, or IEEE Xplore to find peer-reviewed journal articles. Look for papers published in reputable journals or conferences related to your research topic.

  • Use keywords and boolean operators (AND, OR, NOT) to narrow down your search.
  • Evaluate the abstracts and conclusions of papers to determine their relevance to your research question.

Evaluate Sources for Credibility

Not all sources are equally credible. Be sure to evaluate your sources by:

  • Looking for citations of the paper in other reputable works.
  • Reviewing the methodology and sample size used in the study.

Once you‚Äôve found relevant papers, it‚Äôs important to organize them effectively:

Cite Your Sources

Proper citation is essential to avoid plagiarism and give credit to the original authors. Make sure to:

  • Follow a consistent citation style (e.g., APA, MLA, Chicago).
  • Use citation generators or management tools to automate the process (e.g., Zotero, Mendeley).
